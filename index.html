Biography

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was a Hindu monk and one of the most influential spiritual leaders of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Born as Narendranath Dutta, he was inspired by the teachings of Sri Ramakrishna and became a monk at the Ramakrishna Mission. He is best known for his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in 1893, where he introduced Hinduism to the Western world and advocated for religious tolerance.

Teachings

Swami Vivekananda emphasized the idea of "realization of the self," and believed that each person has the potential to realize their own divinity through spiritual practice. He also emphasized the importance of service to others and the oneness of all religions. Some of his most famous quotes include:

  • "Arise, awake, and stop not until the goal is reached."
  • "The whole secret of existence is to have no fear."
  • "The more we come out and do good to others, the more our hearts will be purified, and God will be in them."

Legacy

Swami Vivekananda's teachings and philosophy have had a lasting impact on Hinduism and spirituality in India and around the world. The Ramakrishna Mission, which he founded, continues to spread his message of spiritual enlightenment and social service. His birthday, January 12th, is celebrated as National Youth Day in India.
